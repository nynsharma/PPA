SOP Title:

Process Plant Operation

SOP Number:

PP-001

Revision Number:

1.0

1. Purpose

To provide standard instructions for the safe and efficient operation of the process plant, ensuring product quality, safety, and environmental compliance.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to all employees operating within the process plant, including shift operators, supervisors, and maintenance personnel.

3. Responsibilities

  • Plant Manager: Overall supervision, implementation, and compliance.
  • Shift Supervisor: Ensures all plant operations are carried out according to SOP.
  • Operators: Perform tasks as per procedure and report abnormalities.
  • Maintenance Team: Carry out routine maintenance and respond to issues as reported.

6. Procedure

6.1 Pre-Startup Checks

  1. Verify all maintenance jobs are completed and clearance is given.
  2. Ensure all valves, switches, and controls are in their designated start positions.
  3. Check levels, temperatures, and pressures on tanks and lines.
  4. Confirm utilities (water, electricity, air, steam, etc.) are available and functional.
  5. Wear proper PPE before entering plant premises.

6.2 Startup Procedure

  1. Initiate control system (DCS/PLC).
  2. Start utility systems (e.g., pumps, heaters, compressors) as per schedule.
  3. Gradually introduce raw materials into the process.
  4. Monitor key parameters continuously (flow, temperature, pressure, etc.).
  5. Log readings and report any abnormality to the supervisor.

6.3 Normal Operation

  1. Maintain process conditions as per SOP or production sheet.
  2. Perform regular sampling and quality control checks.
  3. Keep area clean and organized (5S principles).
  4. Report equipment faults or process deviations immediately.

6.4 Shutdown Procedure

  1. Gradually stop raw material feed.
  2. Shut down equipment in a reverse sequence of the startup.
  3. Depressurize and drain lines as per safety standards.
  4. Log all shutdown activities.
  5. Perform post-shutdown inspection and housekeeping.

7. Emergency Procedures

  • Follow the Emergency Response Plan for situations like fire, gas leak, chemical spill, power failure.
  • Use emergency shutdown (ESD) procedures if needed.
  • Evacuate to assembly point and report to safety officer.
